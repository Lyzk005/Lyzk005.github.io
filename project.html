Project

School Life

  
Keys to Control and Continue the Story
  
- w/up s/down a/left d/right - 1/2/3 for answer stage


This project explores everyday life in Vietnamese schools through a typical school day.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

We start early, usually between 5 and 6 a.m., so overcoming sleepiness is a normal part of the day.

Discipline and respect guide routines; students stand up and complete tasks at set times.

Everyone takes a lunch nap, whether a student or not.

Electronic devices are rarely used, and most activities are done by hand, with writing playing an important role.

Students need to be responsible for their classroom and get ready before the teacher arrives.

Learning often relies on memorization, where recalling information exactly is needed for a perfect score.

Each class has a shared notebook for teacher notes; breaking rules may result in consequences.

Cleaning the schoolyard is a common punishment, designed to be visible rather than physically demanding.

Schools are often built vertically, with the yard at the center, visible from upper floors, making actions easily observed.
